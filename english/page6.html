Destroyed the kingdom of Yadava. After Malik Kapur returned to Delhi, Harpaldeva re-established the kingdom of Devagiri in the name of his wife Virve Ikrun. Tried to challenge the power of the Delhi Sultanate in the south by making the Hindu kings of the south loyal. But in 1240 CE, Sultan Mubarak Haya of Delhi attacked. Harpaladeva was not able to face the Sultana's demands as the southern Hindavi king did not help Harpaladeva in time. Harpal Deva was defeated and captured. Mubarak killed him with Kurat. Harpaldeva had protected his brother Karnadeva, king of Gujarat and his son Harandeva by giving them jagir watan at Nandurbar, Karandeva's brother Somdev had done the job of protecting his minor son. Bhindev was married to Rivikadevi, daughter of Raja Jaisingh Parmar of Malwa. He lived in Batapi. On the command of Harpaldev, he successfully brought back Devaldevi, the beautiful sister of Harandeva, from Verul, under the control of the Muslims. Alauddinba raised the general Malik Kapur as Dewal Devi and she was married to Krishna Rao but when she became fit, he was ordered to increase his territory.

Deval Devi was suddenly surrounded by the Mughal army in a cave at Verul. At that time, Bhimdev took Daulat Devi's royal robes, clothes, etc. with his maidservants and fled to Devagiri with his army. Devaldevi herself disguised herself as a farmer's daughter and took a bullock cart in the opposite direction. Somdev alias Govinddev was married to Mahadevi, daughter of Maurya Narasimhadeva of Konkan. He lived in Sundarpur and Sangameshwar. He his brother Bhimdev with the help of Ba, they defeated the Muslims in 1253 AD and captured Goa again. In 1249 AD, Pohammad Tughlaq, the king of Hastanapura marched on Goma and attacked Chandrapur. As he did not join the army of Muhammad Shah, he forfeited his rights. Mangudev alias Nargu Dev, son of Bandhdev, son of Sopeswar, lived in Badami. Kesavadeva alias Nanobdev lived at Anagiri in Karnataka. Kamadeva lived in Sangameshwar. Some of his share was in Karanjul area. His son Someshwar alias Somdev lived there. As Haraladeva's son Narayadeva was a minor, Harandeva's cousin Somabhadev brought him up as a son.

Narayanadev was married to Mahagauri, daughter of Mahadev Yadav. This Miraj lived in Umberaj village near by. Haya unsuccessfully tried to get back his kingdom of Gujarat with the help of Karandev's son Harandeva and Bahimanshah and Rathod Raja Mansingh of Baglan. The relationship between the Chalukya dynasty of Kalyani and Gujarat was strengthened. Befriended Hasan Ganguly. The rights of Kalyani Badami were also recovered. With the consent of Hariappa, Mangudev went to live there after repairing Badami fort. Three sons to Somdev alias Govinddev. Viratryasraya, Shaktideva and Chandradeva Virrajyasraya were married to Padambadevi, daughter of Nagdev Scindia of Sambha. He lived in Kalyani but Hasan Ganguly besieged Goa for six months with his vast army. At that time, the Bahmani Sultan had to defy the Mandlikship and help Shaktidev and Chandradev in the protection of Goa. The three brothers were entangled in the Tirawand Detha by the Muslims, they made a condition of resistance, Radakatideva came to power in 1274, Goa fell under the Bahmani Saro. A few days later, Amber Khan launched a major attack on Kalyan. There was a fierce battle for fifty days. Virasthreya fought bravely. As per the agreement, the traditional rights of Kalyan were retained, but some of the natives had to miss out.

Sundardev was married to Karlika Devi, daughter of Jaisingh Parmar of Malwa. As he had no children, his father's brother's second son Govinddev became his successor. He lived in Goa. Mangudeva's son Govinda joined the war with Kesavadeva alias Visnanobdev's son Gujbandeva in 1278 CE. As he did not have any children, Gujdev considered Manikdev, the son of Harandeva of Gujarat, as his own son. So he made his watnatha entitled. Vishaldev, son of Harandeva's cousin Somabhadev alias Somdev, succeeded Gombiddev. Somabhadeva protected Harpaldeva's son well when he was a minor, thus the family grew together as one family. Kamadeva's son Someshwar alias Somdev Narayan Dev's cousin lived in Karjur. He conquered Anegovu in 1254 CE, the site of the strong and Kajbhuj fort. In connivance with Sirvalal Lahanpayanayak and Buka in the forest here
