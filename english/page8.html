Iman kept it close to him and gave him the right to welfare, played with him. Tawaji Sardari Dipali. In his childhood, he decided to take a job from his cousin. Later this tradition was maintained. Because the father of this family, the main man himself, was not serving any king, he was given leave from the court of Nazrana etc. on request. Kondinadeva was married to Durgadevi, daughter of Yadav of Hatnur. In 1365 AD, Deva Rai of Vijayanagar and Allauddin Multan fought a war over Mudgal and Raipur territories. Bankapur was attacked. Devaraya's eldest son went to Bharla. In saving them, Kondiba's grandfather Katiyardev was injured and later died of pain. In 1368 CE, Miya Naminullah launched the Konkani expedition. The Hindu king was captured. They were forced to become Muslims or die.

Shukya prepared to convert Sarangadeva, the son of King Virattreya of Sangameshwara, into a Muslim. He gave the secret news to Sarangadeva. Sarangdev brought together his brother Sage Soyre and planted Shirkya's fi Turi. North Tuja was attacked at night and eight to ten thousand Muslims were taken away from Kadiya. Kondiba Dev's brothers Shankutadeva, Viradeva came into action in the battle. In this war of 1368, the Chalukya dynasty suffered a great loss. Men of merit were killed and their lands usurped. Sarangadeva alias Virmanobadev hi began to look after the land of his poor family and he later became the heir of Kamadeva, the son of Somdev. Konkan became Mandaleshwar in Karnataka. He was married to Padmuladevi, the only daughter of Surdev of Sangameshwar. Before that, they were able to regain their former homeland from the enemy. As Sarangdeva had no son, Kondiba's third son Madhavji succeeded him. He was married to Shankar Raidhi daughter Suggaladevi (Sumangaladevi). He lived in Konkan, Karnataka region.

Madhavji's four sons, Naroji, lived in Badami. Another Tolak lived in Bankapur. The third Sabaji lived in Gondya. He was married to the only daughter of Haujavar. The fourth Tikoni (Lekavati) was married to the son of Kulambi of Honavar. He used to live at Haujawar. He was an officer in the armory of the Raya of Vijayanagar. Vishaldev is also called Karanadev. He lived at Sangameshwar in Konkan. As his son Jagdev alias Vetungidev got some villages of Mortarap on Ghatmat, he started living in Samangadhi and Bhudargarh. As his son Somdev was killed in a mutual fight in Konkan, his son Bogidev started to build a fort at Solapur near Belgaon. As his territory in Konkan was captured by the enemy, Kondiba gave some of his land to Kardevadi Padami and Marat (Miraj) for the livelihood of his children. Kodibadev's elder son Kamloni was married to Bhetuji Yadav's daughter Mangaladev. In the battle of Kondibadev Bankapur, Chikkarai was killed in a skirmish when he was going to hide Vir Devarai of Vijayanagar in the Janakhana of Chikkarai fort. Kondiba was wounded and died in 1369.

As his three sons, Kamaloji, Skandadev and Madhoji, were minors, the Raya of Vijayanagar appointed Manjana Gosavi and Subhana's son Timoji as administrators in Bankapur Goa and other territories. Later the same period remained in effect. Kalyani was borrowed from the Mahata by the Raya of Vijayanagar. So on his advice Manjana Gosavi was appointed as Amaldar to represent the Chalukya dynasty. It is evident from the documents that this mahant was a well-wisher of Ghalukya Khanwa, and he has been faithfully providing financial and other help to the congregation of this family. All the rest of the system, henceforth or family ownership rights were previously held by the chief rulers of the area. Bamani Sultans Kondibadev was included in the Raya of Vijayanagar, Allauddin Ahmad entrusted his country, Kamalodis was sheltered by the Raya of Vijayanagar, Muhammad Gavan of Konkan defeated the Raja of Khelja in 1391 and besieged Sangameshwar. At that time, Kamaloji had a fierce fight with Bhaubanda. They could not face Mohammad Gavan's army. Arakha Konkan Bhag directed by Mohammad Gavan. Gone was also captured in 1393. Kamologis had to pay all rights and entitlements thereto. Kalyani's minor patrimonial rights to Bijapur remained intact. After Sarangdeva's death, he began to reside in Bankapur for a long time from 1395 onwards.
